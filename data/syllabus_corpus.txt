Introduction to speech processing and speech understanding systems.
The acoustic model maps features to phonemes using hidden markov models.
Mel frequency cepstral coefficients are computed from the power spectrum of audio.
The cepstrum is the inverse fourier transform of the log magnitude spectrum.
Stochastic models include gaussian mixture models and hidden markov models.
The viterbi algorithm finds the most likely state sequence in an hmm.
Constrained beam search uses a language model to restrict hypotheses during decoding.
Pre-emphasis filter boosts high frequency components before windowing the signal.
Hamming window reduces spectral leakage in short time fourier transform analysis.
Fundamental frequency or pitch is extracted using autocorrelation methods.
Formant frequencies define the resonance characteristics of the vocal tract.
Coarticulation refers to the influence of adjacent phonemes on each other.
Prosody includes pitch duration and loudness variations in spoken language.
Code switching occurs when speakers alternate between two or more languages.
Language identification classifies speech segments by the language being spoken.
Kneser-ney smoothing addresses the data sparsity problem in n-gram language models.
Word error rate measures the edit distance between hypothesis and reference transcript.
The connectionist temporal classification loss handles sequence to sequence alignment.
Wav2vec learns speech representations through self-supervised contrastive learning.
The conformer architecture combines convolution and self-attention mechanisms.
Speaker verification uses d-vector or x-vector embeddings for identity comparison.
Dynamic time warping aligns two sequences with different temporal lengths optimally.
Zero-shot voice cloning synthesises speech in a new voice without additional training.
The mel-cepstral distortion measures quality of synthesized audio against reference.
Adversarial examples are imperceptible perturbations that fool machine learning classifiers.
Fast gradient sign method computes adversarial perturbations from the loss gradient sign.
Anti-spoofing systems detect synthetic or replayed speech using countermeasure models.
Linear frequency cepstral coefficients differ from mfcc in the spacing of filter banks.
Equal error rate balances false acceptance and false rejection rates at optimal threshold.
Spectral subtraction removes stationary noise from speech signals using noise estimates.
Deep learning models for speech recognition use encoder decoder transformer architectures.
The attention mechanism allows models to focus on relevant parts of the input sequence.
Speaker diarization segments audio by speaker identity using clustering algorithms.
Voice activity detection identifies regions of speech versus silence in an audio stream.
Pitch detection algorithms include autocorrelation yin pyin and praat methods.
The short time fourier transform computes the spectrum of overlapping audio frames.
Filterbank features are computed by applying triangular filters to the power spectrum.
Delta features capture temporal dynamics of static cepstral coefficients over time.
Neural language models use recurrent or transformer architectures for sequence modeling.
Beam search explores multiple hypotheses simultaneously during sequence decoding.
The baum-welch algorithm performs expectation maximization training for hidden markov models.
Emission probabilities model the likelihood of observations given hidden states.
Transition probabilities model the dynamics between hidden states in a markov chain.
Phoneme recognition is the task of identifying discrete speech sounds from audio.
Grapheme to phoneme conversion maps written characters to phonetic pronunciations.
End to end speech recognition systems directly map audio to text without explicit phonemes.
The encoder maps input audio features to a latent representation for decoding.
Subword units such as byte pair encoding handle out of vocabulary words in speech systems.
Language model shallow fusion combines acoustic model scores with external language model scores.
Speaker adaptation fine-tunes acoustic models for a specific speaker using limited data.
Transfer learning leverages pretrained representations for low resource speech tasks.
Self-supervised pretraining on unlabeled audio improves downstream speech recognition performance.
Multilingual models share parameters across languages to improve low resource performance.
Code-switched speech contains alternating segments of two or more languages in one utterance.
Hinglish is a form of code-switched speech combining Hindi and English vocabulary and grammar.
The matrix language framework describes grammatical constraints in code-switched utterances.
Embedded language islands are phrases from the non-matrix language inserted into utterances.
Language boundaries in code-switched speech occur at morpheme clause and phrase levels.
Bilingual lexical access involves parallel activation of words from two language systems.
